Most of what you read about selling an inherited house is theory. This is what it actually looks like — a real sale, at a real address, with the seller's own words about how it went.

**The short version: an inherited 3-bedroom home at 339 Lawrie Street in Perth Amboy just sold for $385,000 — $35,000 over the asking price.**

![Just Sold: 339 Lawrie Street, Perth Amboy NJ — sold for $385,000, $35,000 over asking](/images/perthAmboy/sold-339-lawrie-st.webp)

## The Situation: A House Nobody Planned On

The family came into this the way most families do — suddenly. They inherited a home in Perth Amboy, and along with the grief came a question they'd never had to answer before: *what do we do with the house?*

Here's how the seller described it in her public Zillow review:

> "We found ourselves in the unexpected position of having to sell a home we inherited. It was an emotional process, and we honestly didn't know which path was the best one for us to take."

That uncertainty is normal. Sell as-is to an investor? Prepare it and list it? Keep it? Every probate mailer in their mailbox was pushing them toward a fast, cheap answer.

## The Result

**339 Lawrie Street — 3 bed, 1.75 bath, 1,524 sq ft — sold for $385,000. That's $35,000 over the asking price.**

This wasn't luck. Perth Amboy is a genuine seller's market right now, and the buyers are there for well-presented homes at this price point — including inherited homes in original condition.

![Perth Amboy NJ market trends, June 2026 — seller's market, median sold price $471K, homes selling at 100.94% of list](/images/perthAmboy/perth-amboy-market-trends-june-2026.webp)

As of June 2026 (Realtors Property Resource, Perth Amboy 08861, single family):

- **3.33 months of inventory** — firmly a seller's market
- Homes selling at **100.94% of list price** on average
- **Median sold price: $471,000**, up month over month

The lesson for estate sellers: the "we buy houses" number on the postcard in your mailbox is not what your inherited home is worth. Competition is.
