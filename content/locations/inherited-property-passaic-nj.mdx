## Probate for a Passaic Estate

Passaic estates go through the **Passaic County Surrogate's Court** in Paterson. While probate runs, the estate carries the property — taxes, insurance, utilities — and in Passaic that often includes a two- or three-family with tenants, which adds rent collection and New Jersey tenant law to the executor's plate. Every estate's path is different; the first step is establishing where yours stands and what the property is honestly worth today.

## What Makes the Passaic Market Different

Passaic packs a big-city rental market into about three square miles: dense blocks of two- and three-family homes, a heavily renter population, NJ Transit rail to New York, and constant demand from families and investors priced out of Bergen County next door. That combination keeps three buyer pools active at once — owner-occupants who need the rental income to qualify, house-hackers, and investors who know the tenant demand never sleeps.

Inherited homes here are typically older multi-family buildings in original condition — exactly what those buyers compete for. The mistake families make is assuming "dated" means "discount."

## Tenants and the Estate

Many Passaic estates come with occupied units. Tenants don't make a building unsellable — investor demand for occupied Passaic multi-families is real — but rent collection, security deposits, and proper notice all have to be handled correctly during probate.
