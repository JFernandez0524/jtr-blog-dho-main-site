**Trenton, New Jersey** — the state capital — is a city with deep history and a real estate market that requires local expertise to navigate. Inheriting property in Trenton means dealing with a unique mix of historic homes, urban neighborhoods, and a probate process centered in Mercer County.

## Probate in Trenton

Trenton is the county seat of Mercer County, so inherited properties go through probate at the **Mercer County Surrogate's Court**, conveniently located at 175 South Broad Street in Trenton itself. This is one of the more accessible Surrogate's Courts in the state — but the process still takes 6–12 months, and the estate is responsible for all carrying costs during that time.

Trenton's property taxes are among the highest in Mercer County, making it especially important to understand your monthly obligations from the moment you inherit.

## Your Options in Trenton

### Sell Traditionally
Trenton's market includes a mix of buyers — first-time homeowners, investors, and buyers priced out of surrounding suburbs like Princeton and Hamilton. Historic neighborhoods like Chambersburg and Hiltonia attract buyers looking for character and value.

### Sell As-Is
Many Trenton properties are older row homes and Victorian-era houses that need significant updating. Selling as-is to an investor or cash buyer is often the most practical path, especially when the estate can't fund repairs.

### Keep the Property
Trenton's rental market is active, driven by state government workers, Rider University students, and families priced out of surrounding suburbs. If the estate can manage a rental, it's worth evaluating.
