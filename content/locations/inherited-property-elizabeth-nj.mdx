**Elizabeth, New Jersey** — the county seat of Union County and one of the state's oldest cities — is a community with a strong working-class identity and a real estate market that has seen significant appreciation in recent years. Inheriting property here means navigating a competitive urban market with real upside.

## Probate in Elizabeth

Elizabeth is the county seat of Union County, so inherited properties go through probate at the **Union County Surrogate's Court**, located at 2 Broad Street in Elizabeth. The process typically takes 6–12 months, and the estate is responsible for all carrying costs during that time.

Elizabeth's property taxes are significant — particularly for multi-family properties — so understanding your monthly obligations from day one is critical.

## Your Options in Elizabeth

### Sell Traditionally
Elizabeth's proximity to Newark Liberty Airport, the New Jersey Turnpike, and NJ Transit's Raritan Valley Line makes it attractive to commuters and investors alike. Neighborhoods like Elmora Hills and North Elizabeth attract buyers looking for value close to major employment centers.

### Sell As-Is
Elizabeth has a large inventory of older multi-family homes and row houses that often need updating. Selling as-is to an investor or cash buyer is a practical option when the estate can't fund repairs or when heirs need a fast resolution.

### Keep the Property
Elizabeth's rental market is strong, driven by its large immigrant community, proximity to Newark Airport, and access to NYC via NJ Transit. Multi-family properties in particular can generate solid cash flow.

## Multi-Family and Tenant Situations
