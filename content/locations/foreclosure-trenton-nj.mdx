## Foreclosure in Mercer County

Trenton is the county seat of Mercer County, so foreclosure cases here move through the **Mercer County Superior Court, Chancery Division**, and a sale — if a case reaches that point — would be conducted through the **Mercer County Sheriff's Office**.

Every homeowner's timeline depends on their specific circumstances. Rather than quote generic numbers, the most useful thing I can offer is an honest look at exactly where you stand.

## Your Options in Trenton

**Protect your equity.** Trenton's mix of historic homes and urban neighborhoods means value varies block by block. If you have equity, selling before the process runs its course protects far more of it than a completed sheriff's sale.

**Sell to a vetted cash buyer.** Trenton has significant older-home and historic-property inventory, sometimes needing real repair work. I maintain a list of vetted investors who pay fair market value — not lowball numbers — and can move faster than a traditional listing.

**Short sale.** If you owe more than the home is worth, a short sale with lender approval typically does far less damage to your credit than a completed foreclosure.

**Loan modification.** If keeping the home is the goal, it's worth exploring a restructured payment plan with your lender before assuming a sale is your only option.

## Watch Out for "Foreclosure Rescue" Offers

Once a foreclosure becomes public record, Trenton homeowners often get targeted with letters and calls promising to "save your home." Be cautious of anyone asking for money upfront or wanting you to sign your deed over — those are scam patterns, not real help.
