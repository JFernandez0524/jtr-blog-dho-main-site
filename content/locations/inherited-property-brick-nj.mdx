Inheriting a home in **Brick Township, New Jersey** puts you in one of Ocean County's most desirable residential communities. Brick's mix of waterfront neighborhoods, established subdivisions, and proximity to the Shore makes it a strong real estate market — but navigating an estate sale here still requires careful planning.

## Probate in Brick Township

Brick Township falls under **Ocean County Surrogate's Court** jurisdiction (118 Washington Street, Toms River). As executor or heir, you'll need to open probate before the property can be transferred or sold. During this period, the estate is responsible for all carrying costs — taxes, insurance, and maintenance.

Brick's property taxes are among the higher ones in Ocean County, so understanding your monthly obligations from day one is critical.

## Your Options in Brick

### Sell Traditionally
Brick's waterfront and near-water properties — particularly along Barnegat Bay and the Metedeconk River — command premium prices. If the home is in good condition and you have time, a traditional listing can maximize your return.

### Sell As-Is
Brick has a large inventory of older ranch-style and cape cod homes that often need updating. Selling as-is is a practical option when the estate can't fund repairs or when heirs want a fast, clean resolution.

### Rent the Property
Brick's proximity to the Shore creates seasonal and year-round rental demand. If the estate can manage a landlord relationship, holding the property as a rental is worth considering.
