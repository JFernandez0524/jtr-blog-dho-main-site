Inheriting a property in **Princeton, New Jersey** can feel both significant and overwhelming. Princeton's real estate market is among the most valuable in the state, which means the decisions you make about an inherited home here carry real financial weight.

## Probate in Princeton

Inherited properties in Princeton typically go through probate in **Mercer County Surrogate's Court**. The process validates the will, appoints an executor, and ultimately transfers ownership to the heirs.

Even during probate, costs continue:

- Property taxes (Princeton has some of the highest in NJ)
- Insurance
- Maintenance and utilities
- Any existing mortgage

Getting clarity on your timeline early can save thousands.

## Your Options for an Inherited Princeton Property

### Sell on the Open Market
Princeton's strong demand means a well-prepared home can command a premium. This path takes more time but may maximize your return.

### Sell As-Is
If the property needs work or you need to settle the estate quickly, selling as-is is a practical option. You skip repairs and close faster — often within weeks.

### Keep or Rent
Given Princeton's rental demand (driven by the university and surrounding employers), some heirs choose to hold the property as an investment.
