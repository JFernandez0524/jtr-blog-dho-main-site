If you've recently inherited a property in **Hamilton, New Jersey**, you're not alone. Hamilton Township is one of Mercer County's largest communities, and families here face the same difficult decisions that come with inheriting a home — often during an already emotional time.

## What Happens to an Inherited Property in Hamilton?

In most cases, inherited property in New Jersey goes through **probate** — a legal process that transfers ownership from the deceased to the heirs. In Hamilton, this is handled through the Mercer County Surrogate's Court.

Probate can take anywhere from a few months to over a year depending on the complexity of the estate. During that time, the property still incurs costs:

- Property taxes
- Homeowner's insurance
- Utilities
- Maintenance

Understanding your timeline early helps you avoid unnecessary expenses.

## Your Options as an Heir in Hamilton

### Keep the Property
Some families choose to keep the home as a residence or rental. Hamilton's location near Trenton and major transit routes can make it a solid long-term hold.

### Sell the Property
If maintaining the home isn't practical — especially with multiple heirs involved — selling may be the right move. You can sell traditionally after repairs, or sell as-is to simplify the process.

### Sell As-Is
Selling as-is means no repairs, no staging, and a faster closing. This is often the best option when the property needs significant work or when heirs need to settle the estate quickly.
