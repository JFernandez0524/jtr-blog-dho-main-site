Facing foreclosure on a home in **Brick Township, New Jersey** doesn't mean you're out of options — it means it's time to understand exactly what those options are.

## Foreclosure in Ocean County

Brick is part of Ocean County, so foreclosure cases here move through the **Ocean County Superior Court, Chancery Division**, and a sale — if a case reaches that point — would be conducted through the **Ocean County Sheriff's Office**.

Every homeowner's timeline looks different depending on where things currently stand with your lender and the court. Rather than quoting generic numbers that may not fit your case, the most useful thing I can do is look at your specific situation and give you an honest answer.

## Your Options in Brick

**Protect your equity.** Brick's mix of waterfront neighborhoods, established subdivisions, and proximity to the Shore has kept it one of Ocean County's more desirable markets. If you have equity, selling before the process runs its course protects far more of it than a completed sheriff's sale.

**Sell to a vetted cash buyer.** Whether the home needs storm-related repairs or just deferred maintenance, I maintain a list of vetted investors who pay fair market value — not lowball numbers — and can move faster than a traditional listing.

**Short sale.** If you owe more than the home is worth, a short sale with lender approval typically causes far less damage to your credit than a completed foreclosure.

**Loan modification.** If keeping the home is the goal, it's worth exploring a restructured payment plan with your lender before assuming a sale is your only path.

## Watch Out for "Foreclosure Rescue" Offers

Once a foreclosure becomes public record, Brick homeowners often get targeted with letters and calls promising to "save your home." Be cautious of anyone asking for money upfront or wanting you to sign your deed over — those are scam patterns, not real help.
