## Foreclosure in Middlesex County

Edison is part of Middlesex County, so foreclosure cases here move through the **Middlesex County Superior Court, Chancery Division**, and a sale — if a case reaches that point — would be conducted through the **Middlesex County Sheriff's Office**.

Every case moves at its own pace depending on your specific circumstances. Rather than quote generic timeframes that may not fit your situation, the most useful thing I can offer is an honest look at exactly where you stand.

## Your Options in Edison

**Protect your equity.** Edison's competitive Central Jersey market has kept demand strong across its many neighborhoods. If you have equity, selling before the process runs its course protects far more of it than a completed sheriff's sale would.

**Sell to a vetted cash buyer.** When speed matters more than maximizing price, I maintain a list of vetted investors who pay fair market value — not lowball numbers — and can move faster than a traditional listing.

**Short sale.** If you owe more than the home is worth, a short sale with lender approval typically does far less damage to your credit than a completed foreclosure.

**Loan modification.** If keeping the home is the goal, it's worth exploring a restructured payment plan with your lender before assuming a sale is your only path.

## Watch Out for "Foreclosure Rescue" Offers

Once a foreclosure becomes public record, Edison homeowners often get targeted with letters and calls promising to "save your home." Be cautious of anyone asking for money upfront or wanting you to sign your deed over — those are scam patterns, not real help.
