**Woodbridge Township, New Jersey** is Middlesex County's most populous municipality and one of the most strategically located communities in the state — sitting at the intersection of the Garden State Parkway and the New Jersey Turnpike. Inheriting property here means you're in a market with strong, consistent demand.

## Probate in Woodbridge

Woodbridge Township falls under **Middlesex County Surrogate's Court** jurisdiction at 75 Bayard Street in New Brunswick. Probate typically takes 6–12 months, and the estate is responsible for all carrying costs during that period — including Woodbridge's property taxes, insurance, and maintenance.

## Your Options in Woodbridge

### Sell Traditionally
Woodbridge's location — with easy access to NYC via NJ Transit's Metropark and Woodbridge stations — makes it attractive to commuters. Neighborhoods like Iselin, Fords, Colonia, and Avenel each have their own character and buyer profiles. A well-priced home here typically moves quickly.

### Sell As-Is
Woodbridge has a large stock of older cape cods, ranches, and split-levels that often need updating. Selling as-is removes the burden of repairs and can close in weeks, which is often the right call for estates that can't fund renovations.

### Keep the Property
Woodbridge's rental market is strong, driven by its central location and transit access. If the estate can manage the property, holding it as a rental is a viable long-term strategy.
