Facing foreclosure on a home in **Hamilton, New Jersey** can feel isolating, but you likely have more options available than you realize — the key is understanding them before deciding anything.

## Foreclosure in Mercer County

Hamilton is part of Mercer County, so foreclosure cases here move through the **Mercer County Superior Court, Chancery Division**, and a sale — if a case reaches that point — would be conducted through the **Mercer County Sheriff's Office**.

Every case moves at its own pace based on your specific circumstances. Rather than quoting generic timeframes, the most useful thing I can offer is an honest look at exactly where you stand and what's still possible.

## Your Options in Hamilton

**Protect your equity.** If you have equity built up in your Hamilton home, selling before the process runs its course protects far more of it than a completed sheriff's sale would.

**Sell to a vetted cash buyer.** When speed matters more than maximizing price, I maintain a list of vetted investors who pay fair market value — not lowball numbers — and can close faster than a traditional listing.

**Short sale.** If you owe more than the home is worth, a short sale with lender approval typically does far less damage to your credit than a completed foreclosure.

**Loan modification.** If keeping the home is the goal, it's worth exploring a restructured payment plan with your lender before assuming a sale is your only option.

## Watch Out for "Foreclosure Rescue" Offers

Once a foreclosure becomes public record, Hamilton homeowners often get targeted with letters and calls promising to "save your home." Be cautious of anyone asking for money upfront or wanting you to sign your deed over — those are scam patterns, not real help.
