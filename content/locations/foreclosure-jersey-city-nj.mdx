**Jersey City, New Jersey** is one of the most valuable real estate markets in the entire state — which means if you're facing foreclosure here, there's likely significant equity worth protecting.

## Foreclosure in Hudson County

Jersey City is the county seat of Hudson County, so foreclosure cases here move through the **Hudson County Superior Court, Chancery Division**, and a sale — if a case reaches that point — would be conducted through the **Hudson County Sheriff's Office**.

Every homeowner's timeline is different depending on where things stand with your lender and the court. Rather than quote generic numbers, the most useful thing I can offer is an honest look at your specific situation.

## Your Options in Jersey City

**Protect your equity.** Jersey City's fast-moving, high-value market means many homeowners here have real equity at stake. If that's you, selling before the process runs its course protects far more of it than a completed sheriff's sale.

**Sell to a vetted cash buyer.** Jersey City's dense mix of condos, multi-families, and rowhomes sometimes comes with tenant or condition complications. I maintain a list of vetted investors who pay fair market value — not lowball numbers — and can move faster than a traditional listing.

**Short sale.** If you owe more than the home is worth, a short sale with lender approval typically does far less damage to your credit than a completed foreclosure.

**Loan modification.** If keeping the home is the priority, it's worth exploring a restructured payment plan with your lender before assuming a sale is your only path.

## Watch Out for "Foreclosure Rescue" Offers

Once a foreclosure becomes public record, Jersey City homeowners often get targeted with letters and calls promising to "save your home." Be cautious of anyone asking for money upfront or wanting you to sign your deed over — those are scam patterns, not real help.
