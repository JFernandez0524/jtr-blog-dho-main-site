**Newark, New Jersey** is the state's largest city and one of the most dynamic real estate markets in the Northeast. Inheriting property here means navigating a complex urban market — but also one with real upside, particularly as Newark continues its revitalization.

## Probate in Newark

Newark is the county seat of Essex County, so inherited properties go through probate at the **Essex County Surrogate's Court**, located at 465 Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. Boulevard in Newark. The Surrogate's Court is one of the busiest in New Jersey, and wait times can be longer than in smaller counties — plan for 9–15 months in complex cases.

Newark's property taxes are among the highest in the state. Understanding your carrying costs from day one is essential.

## Your Options in Newark

### Sell Traditionally
Newark's market has transformed significantly over the past decade. Neighborhoods like the Ironbound, Forest Hill, and the North Ward attract buyers looking for value close to New York City. A well-priced home in the right neighborhood can generate strong interest.

### Sell As-Is
Newark has a large inventory of older multi-family homes, row houses, and Victorian-era properties that often need significant work. Selling as-is to an investor is frequently the most practical path, especially for properties with deferred maintenance or tenant complications.

### Keep the Property
Newark's rental market is one of the strongest in New Jersey, driven by proximity to NYC, Newark Liberty Airport, and major employers like Prudential and NJIT. Multi-family properties in particular can generate strong cash flow.

## Multi-Family and Tenant Situations

Many Newark estates involve multi-family properties with existing tenants. New Jersey's tenant protection laws are among the strongest in the country — I help families understand their obligations and options before making any decisions.
