**Paterson, New Jersey** — New Jersey's third-largest city and the county seat of Passaic County — is a community with deep roots and a real estate market that rewards local knowledge. Inheriting property in Paterson means navigating a diverse urban market with unique opportunities and challenges.

## Probate in Paterson

Paterson is the county seat of Passaic County, so inherited properties go through probate at the **Passaic County Surrogate's Court**, located at 77 Hamilton Street in Paterson. The process typically takes 6–12 months, and the estate is responsible for all carrying costs during that time.

Paterson's property taxes are among the highest in Passaic County — understanding your monthly obligations from the moment you inherit is essential.

## Your Options in Paterson

### Sell Traditionally
Paterson's market includes a mix of buyers — first-time homeowners, investors, and buyers priced out of surrounding suburbs like Wayne and Clifton. Historic neighborhoods like Eastside and the Great Falls Historic District attract buyers looking for character and value.

### Sell As-Is
Paterson has a large inventory of older multi-family homes and row houses that often need significant updating. Selling as-is to an investor or cash buyer is frequently the most practical path, especially when the estate can't fund repairs.

### Keep the Property
Paterson's rental market is active, driven by its large working-class population and proximity to major employers. Multi-family properties in particular can generate consistent rental income.
