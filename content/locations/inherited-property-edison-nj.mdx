**Edison, New Jersey** is one of Middlesex County's largest and most diverse townships — and one of the most competitive real estate markets in Central Jersey. Inheriting property here means you have real options, but also real decisions to make quickly.

## Probate in Edison

Edison Township falls under **Middlesex County Surrogate's Court** jurisdiction, located at 75 Bayard Street in New Brunswick. Probate typically takes 6–12 months, during which the estate is responsible for Edison's property taxes, insurance, and maintenance costs.

Edison's property taxes are significant — particularly in neighborhoods like North Edison and Raritan Center — so understanding your carrying costs from day one is critical.

## Your Options in Edison

### Sell Traditionally
Edison's strong school system, diverse community, and central location between New York and Philadelphia create consistent buyer demand. Neighborhoods like Menlo Park Terrace, Clara Barton, and Stelton attract buyers willing to pay a premium for move-in ready homes.

### Sell As-Is
Edison has a large inventory of split-level and ranch homes from the 1960s–1980s that often need updating. Selling as-is is practical when the estate can't fund renovations or when heirs need a fast, clean resolution.

### Keep the Property
Edison's rental market is among the strongest in Central Jersey, driven by proximity to major employers, Rutgers University, and easy access to NYC via NJ Transit. Holding the property as a rental is worth serious consideration.
