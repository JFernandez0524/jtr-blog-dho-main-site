## Why Elizabeth Foreclosures Have More at Stake Than Most

Elizabeth has appreciated significantly in recent years — from Elmora and Westminster to Peterstown, Elizabethport, and North Elizabeth, values have climbed while many longtime owners' mortgage balances haven't. That combination means homeowners in default here often have **real equity on the line**. A completed sheriff's sale is the worst possible way to lose it: fees and interest pile onto the judgment while auction bidding runs below market. Selling before that point — on your terms — is how you keep what's yours.

As the county seat, Elizabeth is home to the **Union County Superior Court, Chancery Division**, where these cases are handled, and the **Union County Sheriff's Office**, which conducts sales. Where your case sits in that process determines your options — and that's a conversation about your specific facts, not a generic chart.

## Paths I Walk Elizabeth Homeowners Through

**Sell at market and walk away with your equity.** Elizabeth's buyer demand — driven by its location, transit, and price point relative to neighboring counties — supports strong sales even for homes that need work.

**Sell fast to a vetted buyer.** Elizabeth's housing stock leans heavily on two-families and older multi-families, sometimes tenant-occupied — situations that scare off retail buyers but not my vetted investor list. These buyers pay fair market value, not the lowball numbers on the "we buy houses" flyers, and they can move quickly when a deadline looms.

**Short sale.** Owe more than it's worth? A lender-approved short sale typically protects your credit far better than a completed foreclosure — and lenders approve them because it costs them less too.

**Loan modification.** If the goal is keeping the home, I'll give you an honest read on whether your numbers support restructuring before you spend months chasing it.
