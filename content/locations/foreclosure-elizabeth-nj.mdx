**Elizabeth, New Jersey** — the county seat of Union County and one of the state's oldest cities — has seen real appreciation in recent years, which means homeowners facing foreclosure here often have more equity at stake than they realize.

## Foreclosure in Union County

As the county seat, Elizabeth is home to the **Union County Superior Court, Chancery Division**, where foreclosure cases here are handled, and a sale — if a case reaches that point — would be conducted through the **Union County Sheriff's Office**.

Every homeowner's timeline depends on their specific circumstances — where things stand with your lender, whether anything's been filed. Rather than quote generic numbers, the most useful thing I can offer is an honest look at your exact situation.

## Your Options in Elizabeth

**Protect your equity.** Elizabeth's competitive urban market has appreciated significantly. If you have equity, selling before the process runs its course protects far more of it than a completed sheriff's sale.

**Sell to a vetted cash buyer.** When speed matters more than maximizing price, I maintain a list of vetted investors who pay fair market value — not lowball numbers — and can move faster than a traditional listing.

**Short sale.** If you owe more than the home is worth, a short sale with lender approval typically does far less damage to your credit than a completed foreclosure.

**Loan modification.** If keeping the home is the priority, it's worth exploring a restructured payment plan with your lender before assuming a sale is your only path.

## Watch Out for "Foreclosure Rescue" Offers

Once a foreclosure becomes public record, Elizabeth homeowners often get targeted with letters and calls promising to "save your home." Be cautious of anyone asking for money upfront or wanting you to sign your deed over — those are scam patterns, not real help.
