Facing foreclosure on a home in **Princeton, New Jersey** doesn't happen often, but when it does, the stakes — and the equity involved — tend to be significant.

## Foreclosure in Mercer County

Princeton is part of Mercer County, so foreclosure cases here move through the **Mercer County Superior Court, Chancery Division**, and a sale — if a case reaches that point — would be conducted through the **Mercer County Sheriff's Office**.

Every homeowner's timeline depends on their specific circumstances. Rather than quote generic numbers, the most useful thing I can offer is an honest look at exactly where you stand.

## Your Options in Princeton

**Protect your equity.** Princeton's market carries some of the highest home values in the state, which means the equity at risk in a completed sheriff's sale can be substantial. Selling before the process runs its course protects far more of that value.

**Sell to a vetted cash buyer.** For older or historic Princeton-area homes needing significant work, I maintain a list of vetted investors who pay fair market value — not lowball numbers — and can move faster than a traditional listing.

**Short sale.** If you owe more than the home is worth, a short sale with lender approval typically does far less damage to your credit than a completed foreclosure.

**Loan modification.** If keeping the home is the priority, it's worth exploring a restructured payment plan with your lender before assuming a sale is your only option.

## Watch Out for "Foreclosure Rescue" Offers

Once a foreclosure becomes public record, homeowners often get targeted with letters and calls promising to "save your home." Be cautious of anyone asking for money upfront or wanting you to sign your deed over — those are scam patterns, not real help, regardless of how the offer is dressed up.
