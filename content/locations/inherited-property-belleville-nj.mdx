## Probate for a Belleville Estate

Belleville estates run through the **Essex County Surrogate's Court** in Newark. While that process moves, the property keeps generating carrying costs — Essex County taxes, insurance, utilities, maintenance — and if it's a two-family with a tenant, there's rent, repairs, and NJ tenant law to manage on top of the legal process. Sorting out who's responsible for what, and what the building is actually worth, is step one. Every estate moves at its own pace, so let's talk about where yours stands rather than guess.

## What Belleville Estates Usually Look Like

Belleville's housing stock is heavy on two-families and solid pre-war homes — from Silver Lake (steps from Newark's Forest Hill and the light rail) up through the township's residential blocks. Inherited homes here are often original-condition, sometimes tenant-occupied, and almost always worth more than the family's first guess: Belleville's price point and transit access keep both first-time buyers and investors circling.

**A tenant in place changes the plan, not the outcome.** An occupied two-family can be sold occupied (investors buy them that way routinely) or after a managed transition — but NJ tenant protections mean the sequencing matters. I help families get this right before anything is signed.
