If you're facing foreclosure on a home in **Toms River, New Jersey**, you're in the county seat and largest municipality in Ocean County — a strong buyer pool that works in your favor if you decide to sell.

## Foreclosure in Ocean County

As the county seat, Toms River is home to the **Ocean County Superior Court, Chancery Division**, where foreclosure cases here are handled, and a sale — if a case reaches that point — would be conducted through the **Ocean County Sheriff's Office**.

Every homeowner's timeline is different depending on where things stand with your lender and the court. Rather than quote generic numbers, the most useful thing I can offer is an honest look at your specific situation.

## Your Options in Toms River

**Protect your equity.** Toms River's active market and strong buyer demand mean many homeowners here have real equity built up. If that's you, selling before the process runs its course protects far more of it than a completed sheriff's sale.

**Sell to a vetted cash buyer.** When speed matters more than maximizing price, I maintain a list of vetted investors who pay fair market value — not lowball numbers — and can move faster than a traditional listing.

**Short sale.** If you owe more than the home is worth, a short sale with lender approval typically does far less damage to your credit than a completed foreclosure.

**Loan modification.** If keeping the home is the priority, it's worth exploring a restructured payment plan with your lender before assuming a sale is your only option.

## Watch Out for "Foreclosure Rescue" Offers

Once a foreclosure becomes public record, Toms River homeowners often get targeted with letters and calls promising to "save your home." Be cautious of anyone asking for money upfront or wanting you to sign your deed over — those are scam patterns, not real help.
